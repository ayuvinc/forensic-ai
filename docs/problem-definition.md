# Problem Definition — GoodWork Forensic AI

_Populated via /ba discovery session — 2026-04-04_

---

## Who

**Primary user:** Maher Hashash, Managing Director, GoodWork LLC (registered UAE).

**Firm structure:** Solo practitioner — no staff, no associates. Maher is simultaneously the analyst, reviewer, and partner on every engagement.

**Clients:** CXOs of client companies (CEO, CFO, COO, board-level). Deliverables must be executive-ready — clear, structured, and professional without unnecessary technical or regulatory jargon.

**Secondary consumers:** Depending on engagement type — legal counsel, regulators, insurers (via persona review).

---

## What

GoodWork provides six service lines:

1. **Forensic Investigation Reports** — fact-finding, evidence analysis, findings narrative
2. **Fraud Risk Management (FRM) Risk Registers** — structured risk identification, rating, and regulatory mapping
3. **Policies and SOPs** — internal compliance documents for clients
4. **Training Material** — role-specific staff training content
5. **Client Proposals** — engagement letters and scope-of-work documents
6. **Proposal Decks** — PowerPoint prompt packs for client presentations

**Current workflow without the tool (all manual):**
1. Client engagement → intake notes (varies by case type)
2. Notes converted to Scope & Approach document for client sign-off
3. Evidence review: email PSTs (Outlook / eDiscovery tools), financial data (Excel), documents (PDFs, Word)
4. Evidence captured as exhibits
5. Word report drafted, structured, and finalized
6. Proposals drafted in Word; decks in PowerPoint

**Time per engagement:** 3–8 weeks from evidence-ready to client-ready draft, depending on scope.

---

## Why

Every stage of the workflow is manual:
- **Evidence organization** — no structured way to capture, index, or retrieve exhibits
- **Report writing** — structure and language built from scratch each time
- **Regulatory references** — looked up and verified manually
- **Audience calibration** — language adapted for CXO, legal, or regulatory readers by hand
- **Review cycles** — Maher reviews his own work without a second perspective

**Core pain:** Getting structure and language right, and not missing any analysis. As a solo practitioner, there is no associate or junior to catch gaps, draft first passes, or standardize language.

**Business constraint:** Hiring associates to handle grunt work is the alternative — expensive and operationally complex for a sole practitioner firm.

---

## Problem Statement

Maher Hashash, a solo forensic consultant, spends 3–8 weeks per engagement on manual writing and organization work — drafting report structure, standardizing findings language, tracking evidence, and calibrating tone for CXO audiences — because every stage is done from scratch with no tooling. This limits his capacity, creates quality variance across engagements, and requires either significant personal time or the cost of associates he cannot justify for a sole-practitioner firm.

---

## What the Tool Does and Does Not Do

**Does:**
- Organize facts and evidence provided by Maher
- Standardize findings language and report structure
- Draft reports, proposals, training material, and policies
- Map findings to regulatory frameworks
- Simulate a second review perspective (PM, Partner, Persona)

**Does NOT:**
- Perform investigations or draw analytical conclusions
- Replace Maher's professional judgment — he provides the facts, evidence, and conclusions
- Produce client-facing output without Maher's review and approval
- Provide legal opinions or act as expert witness
